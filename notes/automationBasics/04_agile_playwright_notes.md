# 🚀 Introduction to Agile (Premium Notes for Automation Engineers)

---

# 1. Traditional Software Development (Waterfall)

## Concept

Waterfall is a **sequential model** where each phase is completed before moving to the next:

- Requirement → Design → Development → Testing → Deployment

---

## Example

A banking app:

- Requirements finalized first
- Development done fully
- Testing happens at the end

---

## ⚡ Important

❌ Late bug detection
❌ No flexibility for changes
❌ High cost of defects

---

# 2. Agile Software Development

## Concept

Agile is an **iterative and incremental approach** where software is developed in small cycles (sprints).

---

## Example

Instead of building full app:

- Sprint 1 → Login feature
- Sprint 2 → Dashboard
- Sprint 3 → Payments

---

## ⚡ Important

🔥 Continuous feedback
🔥 Faster releases
🔥 Perfect for startups

---

# 3. Agile Manifesto

## Concept

Core values:

- Individuals over processes
- Working software over documentation
- Customer collaboration over contracts
- Responding to change over following plan

---

## ⚡ Important

- Focus on **value delivery**
- Avoid over-documentation

---

# 4. Agile Principles (Key Understanding)

## Concept

12 principles focus on:

- customer satisfaction
- continuous delivery
- embracing change

---

## ⚡ Important

- Frequently asked in interviews
- Core philosophy behind Agile

---

# 5. Agile vs Waterfall

## Concept

Waterfall → fixed, sequential
Agile → flexible, iterative

---

## Example

Waterfall → 6 months release
Agile → releases every 2 weeks

---

## ⚡ Important

- Most SaaS companies use Agile

---

# 6. SCRUM Framework (MOST IMPORTANT)

## Concept

Scrum is the most widely used Agile framework.

---

## Key Roles

### Product Owner

- Defines requirements

### Scrum Master

- Removes blockers

### Development Team

- Builds + tests product

---

## ⚡ Important

- Tester is part of dev team (not separate)

---

# 7. Scrum Artifacts

## Concept

Key items:

- Product Backlog → all requirements
- Sprint Backlog → sprint tasks
- Increment → working software

---

## Example

Login feature in backlog → picked in sprint → delivered

---

## ⚡ Important

- Backlog grooming is critical

---

# 8. Scrum Events

## Sprint

Time-boxed iteration (2 weeks)

## Daily Standup

Team sync (15 min)

## Sprint Planning

Decide sprint work

## Sprint Review

Demo to stakeholders

## Retrospective

Improve process

---

## ⚡ Important

🔥 Retrospective = biggest improvement tool

---

# 9. User Stories

## Concept

Format:
"As a user, I want X so that Y"

---

## Example

"As a user, I want to login so that I can access dashboard"

---

## ⚡ Important

- Must be testable
- Should have acceptance criteria

---

# 10. Definition of Done (DoD)

## Concept

Criteria to mark work complete

---

## Example

- Code written
- Tested
- Deployed

---

## ⚡ Important

- Avoid incomplete delivery

---

# 11. Story Points & Estimation

## Concept

Effort estimation using story points

---

## Example

Login → 3 points
Payment → 8 points

---

## ⚡ Important

- Relative estimation (not time-based)

---

# 12. Planning Poker

## Concept

Team estimates story points collaboratively

---

## ⚡ Important

- Reduces bias
- Improves team alignment

---

# 13. Kanban

---

## Example

Task board tracking automation scripts

---

## ⚡ Important

- Continuous flow (no sprints)

---

# 14. Extreme Programming (XP)

Focus on:

- code quality

- continuous integration

---

## ⚡ Important

# 15. Agile Testing

## Concept

Testing happens continuously in Agile

---

## Example

Tester works with developer during sprint

---

## ⚡ Important

🔥 No separate testing phase
🔥 Testing = continuous

---

# 16. Role of Tester in Agile

## Concept

Tester responsibilities:

- write test cases
- automate tests
- collaborate with devs

---

## ⚡ Important

- Tester becomes **quality engineer**

---

# 17. Agile vs Traditional Testing

## Concept

Traditional → testing after dev
Agile → testing during dev

---

## ⚡ Important

- Faster feedback cycle

---

# 18. Agile Testing Quadrants

- Exploratory tests

## ⚡ Important

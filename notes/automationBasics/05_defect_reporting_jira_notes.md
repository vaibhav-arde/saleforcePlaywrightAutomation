# 1. Software Quality

## Concept
Software quality means how well the application meets:
- Requirements
- User expectations
- Performance standards

---

## Example
A login feature:
- Works correctly → functional quality  
- Loads fast → performance quality  

---

## ⚡ Important
- Quality ≠ No bugs  
- Quality = fitness for use  

---

# 2. Defect (Bug) Definition

## Concept
A defect is a mismatch between expected and actual result.

---

## Example
Expected: Login successful  
Actual: Error shown  

---

## ⚡ Important
- Always compare with requirements

---

# 3. Why Find Defects?

## Concept
Finding defects ensures:
- Product reliability
- Customer satisfaction
- Reduced cost

---

## Example
Payment failure bug → revenue loss  

---

## ⚡ Important
🔥 Critical for SaaS retention  

---

# 5. Defect Life Cycle

## Concept
Typical flow:
New → Assigned → Open → Fixed → Retest → Closed → Reopen  

---

## Example
Tester logs bug → Dev fixes → Tester verifies  

---

## ⚡ Important
- Must understand for Jira workflows  

---

# 6. Defect Report

## Concept
A document describing a bug clearly

---

## Example (Template)
- Title  
- Description  
- Steps to reproduce  
- Expected result  
- Actual result  
- Severity & Priority  

---

## ⚡ Important
🔥 Clear defect = faster fix  

---

# 7. Severity vs Priority

## Concept
- Severity → impact  
- Priority → urgency  

---

## Example
App crash:
- Severity = High  
- Priority = High  

UI typo:
- Severity = Low  
- Priority = Low  

---

## ⚡ Important
- Interview favorite  
- Don’t confuse both  

---

# 8. Good vs Bad Defect Report

## Concept
Good report:
- clear steps  
- reproducible  
- evidence  

Bad report:
- vague  
- incomplete  

---

## Example
Bad: "Login not working"  
Good: "Login fails with valid credentials, error message shown"

---

## ⚡ Important
- Quality of report = team efficiency  

---
